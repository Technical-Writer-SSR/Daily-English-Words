# Daily English Words Repository

**Date:** 09 December 2025  
**Word of the Day:** **Yawn** (/jɔːn/) • verb  

**Meaning:**  
To open the mouth involuntarily while taking a deep breath, usually due to tiredness, sleepiness, or boredom. Can also describe something boring.

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

- **Morning Routine:** "I yawned as soon as I woke up and stretched in bed."  
- **Workplace:** "He tried to hide a yawn during the long meeting."  
- **Classroom:** "The lecture was so dull that students kept yawning."  
- **Travel:** "She yawned on the train, tired from the early morning journey."  
- **Social Interaction:** "He stifled a yawn while listening to the story."  
- **Health Observation:** "Excessive yawning can sometimes indicate fatigue or stress."  
- **Nighttime Routine:** "Babies often yawn before falling asleep."  
- **Boredom:** "I yawned as I flipped through the monotonous report."  
- **Sports or Exercise:** "He yawned after finishing his early morning jog."  
- **Travel Companion:** "She noticed her friend yawning repeatedly during the long flight."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **yawn** as the sound you make when your mouth opens wide and your body signals *tiredness or boredom*.

---

### Synonyms
stretch, gape, open wide, sigh, exhale  

### Antonyms
alertness, wakefulness, attentiveness  

---

### Word Forms
- **Yawned** (past tense): "He yawned during the movie."  
- **Yawning** (present participle): "She is yawning because she is sleepy."  
- **Yawn** (noun): "A big yawn escaped him."

---

### Common Phrases
- Yawn and stretch  
- Suppress a yawn  
- A big yawn  
- Yawning widely
