## Word of the Day: **Recognition**
/ˌrek.əɡˈnɪʃ.ən/ • noun  

The act of identifying or acknowledging someone or something; appreciation or acceptance of importance.

---

### Meaning
The act of recognizing or identifying someone or something; acknowledgment, appreciation, or approval for achievements or qualities.

**Part of Speech:** noun

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Workplace Appreciation:**  
   "She received recognition from her manager for completing the project on time."

2. **Education:**  
   "The student gained recognition for his excellent performance in exams."

3. **Technology Usage:**  
   "My phone uses face recognition to unlock the screen."

4. **Public Events:**  
   "The artist received public recognition during the award ceremony."

5. **Daily Interaction:**  
   "He smiled in recognition when he saw his old friend at the bus stop."

6. **Office Environment:**  
   "Employees feel motivated when their hard work gets recognition."

7. **Security System:**  
   "Voice recognition is used to verify the user's identity."

8. **Social Media:**  
   "Her work gained recognition after it was shared online."

9. **Family Life:**  
   "Children need recognition for their efforts, not just their results."

10. **Health Sector:**  
    "Early recognition of symptoms can help prevent serious illness."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **recognition** as **“re + cognition”** — knowing or identifying something *again*.

---

### Synonyms
acknowledgment, appreciation, identification, awareness, acceptance, credit

### Antonyms
ignorance, neglect, denial, disregard, rejection

---

### Word Forms
- **Recognize** (verb): "I recognize his voice."
- **Recognized** (past tense): "She was recognized for her contribution."
- **Recognizing** (present participle): "He is recognizing the problem early."

---

### Common Phrases
- Gain recognition
- Face recognition
- Voice recognition
- Public recognition
- Recognition system
