**Date:** 17 December 2025

## Word of the Day: **Accomplish**
/əˈkʌm.plɪʃ/ • verb  

To successfully complete something; to achieve a goal or task.

---

### Meaning
To successfully carry out or complete a task, goal, or activity; to achieve something through effort.

**Part of Speech:** verb

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Work Goals:**  
   "She accomplished all her tasks before the end of the day."

2. **Education:**  
   "He accomplished his goal of finishing the course with top grades."

3. **Personal Projects:**  
   "I accomplished building my own bookshelf over the weekend."

4. **Fitness Goals:**  
   "She accomplished running 5 kilometers without stopping."

5. **Teamwork:**  
   "The team accomplished the project ahead of schedule."

6. **Daily Life:**  
   "I accomplished grocery shopping, cooking, and cleaning all before noon."

7. **Learning Skills:**  
   "He accomplished learning the guitar after months of practice."

8. **Professional Achievements:**  
   "The manager accomplished the quarterly targets successfully."

9. **Travel Goals:**  
   "We accomplished visiting all the landmarks during our trip."

10. **Creative Work:**  
    "The artist accomplished finishing the painting just before the exhibition."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **accomplish** as **“a + accomplish”** — completing or achieving *a* goal.

---

### Synonyms
achieve, complete, finish, succeed, realize, attain, execute

### Antonyms
fail, abandon, neglect, give up, forfeit

---

### Word Forms
- **Accomplished** (past tense/adjective): "She accomplished the task yesterday."  
- **Accomplishing** (present participle): "He is accomplishing his goals step by step."

---

### Common Phrases
- Accomplish a task  
- Accomplish a goal  
- Accomplish a mission  
- Accomplish a project  
- Accomplish objectives
