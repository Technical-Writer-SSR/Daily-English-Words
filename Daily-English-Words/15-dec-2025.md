## Word of the Day: **Considerate**
/kənˈsɪd.ər.ət/ • adjective  

Careful not to inconvenience or harm others; thoughtful of the feelings and needs of others.

---

### Meaning
Being mindful of others’ feelings, needs, or comfort; showing thoughtfulness and kindness in actions.

**Part of Speech:** adjective

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Workplace Courtesy:**  
   "He was considerate enough to wait for his colleague before starting the meeting."

2. **Public Behavior:**  
   "She is always considerate when using her phone in public places."

3. **Family Life:**  
   "Being considerate, he helped his younger sibling with homework."

4. **Travel:**  
   "Passengers appreciated the considerate announcement about the delay."

5. **Friendship:**  
   "A considerate friend always listens carefully and offers support."

6. **Daily Interaction:**  
   "He opened the door for the elderly lady—a considerate gesture."

7. **Healthcare:**  
   "The nurse was considerate and explained the procedure calmly to the patient."

8. **Social Events:**  
   "Being considerate, she made sure everyone had enough food and drinks."

9. **Community Life:**  
   "Considerate neighbors keep the noise down late at night."

10. **Work Assignments:**  
    "He was considerate in assigning tasks according to team members’ strengths."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **considerate** as **“consider + ate”** — always *consider* others before acting.

---

### Synonyms
thoughtful, kind, mindful, attentive, courteous, respectful

### Antonyms
inconsiderate, rude, thoughtless, selfish, careless

---

### Word Forms
- **Consider** (verb): "Always consider others’ opinions."
- **Consideration** (noun): "She showed great consideration for her colleagues."
- **Considerately** (adverb): "He acted considerately towards everyone."

---

### Common Phrases
- Be considerate
- Considerate behavior
- Considerate of others
- Thoughtful and considerate
