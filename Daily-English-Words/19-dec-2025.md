## Word of the Day: **Notable**
/ˈnoʊ.t̬ə.bəl/ • adjective  

Worth noticing or remarkable; deserving attention or recognition.

---

### Meaning
Deserving attention or notice; remarkable, significant, or noteworthy.

**Part of Speech:** adjective

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Work Achievement:**  
   "Her contribution to the project was notable and appreciated by the team."

2. **Education:**  
   "He made notable progress in his English writing over the past month."

3. **Events:**  
   "The ceremony featured notable speakers from various industries."

4. **Daily Interaction:**  
   "It was notable that he remembered everyone's birthday."

5. **Art and Culture:**  
   "The gallery displayed notable works of modern art."

6. **Sports:**  
   "The player had a notable performance during the championship."

7. **News or Media:**  
   "The article highlighted a notable change in local policies."

8. **Science & Technology:**  
   "A notable discovery in renewable energy was published recently."

9. **Community Work:**  
   "Her notable efforts in volunteering inspired others."

10. **Personal Life:**  
    "It was notable how calm he remained during the stressful situation."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **notable** as **“note + able”** — something *able to be noted or remembered*.

---

### Synonyms
remarkable, noteworthy, significant, outstanding, important, impressive

### Antonyms
insignificant, unremarkable, ordinary, minor, trivial

---

### Word Forms
- **Notably** (adverb): "He is notably skilled in negotiation."
- **Notate** (verb, less common): "She notated the music carefully."

---

### Common Phrases
- Notable achievement  
- Notable event  
- Notable contribution  
- Particularly notable  
- Notable figure
