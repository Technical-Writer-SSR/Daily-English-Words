## Word of the Day: **Misreading**
/ˌmɪsˈriː.dɪŋ/ • noun  

An incorrect understanding or interpretation of something that is read, seen, or heard.

---

### Meaning
A wrong or mistaken interpretation or understanding, especially of written text, instructions, or situations.

**Part of Speech:** noun

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Workplace Communication:**  
   "The delay happened because of a misreading of the project instructions."

2. **Academic Study:**  
   "A small misreading of the exam question led to the wrong answer."

3. **Daily Messaging:**  
   "The argument started due to a misreading of the text message."

4. **Technical Field:**  
   "The system failure was caused by a misreading of sensor data."

5. **Travel Situation:**  
   "We missed the train because of a misreading of the timetable."

6. **Medical Context:**  
   "The doctor corrected a misreading of the test results."

7. **Office Documentation:**  
   "A misreading of the contract caused confusion between the teams."

8. **Navigation:**  
   "The wrong turn was due to a misreading of the map."

9. **Social Interaction:**  
   "His reaction was based on a misreading of her tone."

10. **Everyday Life:**  
    "Locking the door twice was a misreading of the warning sign."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **misreading** as **“mis + reading”** — reading something *wrong*.

---

### Synonyms
misinterpretation, misunderstanding, error, mistake, misjudgment

### Antonyms
understanding, correct interpretation, clarity, comprehension

---

### Word Forms
- **Misread** (verb): "I misread the email."
- **Misread** (past tense): "She misread the instructions."
- **Misreading** (noun): "That was a misreading of the rules."

---

### Common Phrases
- A misreading of facts
- Misreading the situation
- Misreading instructions
- Misreading signals
