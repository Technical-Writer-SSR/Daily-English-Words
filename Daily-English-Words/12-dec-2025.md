## Word of the Day: **Enthusiasm**
/ɪnˈθuː.zi.æz.əm/ • noun  

Intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval; strong excitement or passion about something.

---

### Meaning
A strong feeling of excitement, eagerness, or passion toward a particular activity, idea, or event.

**Part of Speech:** noun

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Workplace Energy:**  
   "She approached the new project with great enthusiasm and creativity."

2. **Learning:**  
   "The student’s enthusiasm for science inspired others in the class."

3. **Hobby Pursuit:**  
   "He showed enthusiasm while painting, spending hours on every detail."

4. **Sports:**  
   "The team played with enthusiasm, cheering each other on throughout the match."

5. **Social Events:**  
   "Her enthusiasm made the birthday party lively and fun."

6. **Travel Planning:**  
   "He expressed enthusiasm when planning the trip to Japan."

7. **Volunteer Work:**  
   "The volunteers worked with enthusiasm to clean up the park."

8. **Creative Arts:**  
   "Her enthusiasm for music was evident in every performance."

9. **Family Activities:**  
   "The children’s enthusiasm for the festival was contagious."

10. **Professional Growth:**  
    "He tackled every new responsibility with enthusiasm, impressing his boss."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **enthusiasm** as **“en + the + us + iasm”** — imagine filling *us* with energy and excitement.

---

### Synonyms
excitement, eagerness, passion, zeal, energy, fervor

### Antonyms
apathy, indifference, disinterest, boredom, dullness

---

### Word Forms
- **Enthusiastic** (adjective): "She is enthusiastic about learning new skills."  
- **Enthusiastically** (adverb): "He enthusiastically joined the team meeting."

---

### Common Phrases
- Show enthusiasm  
- Enthusiasm for learning  
- Enthusiasm at work  
- Full of enthusiasm  
- Infectious enthusiasm
