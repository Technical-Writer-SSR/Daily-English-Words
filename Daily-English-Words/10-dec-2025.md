## Word of the Day: **Perceive**
/pərˈsiːv/ • verb  

To become aware of, understand, or interpret something through the senses or the mind.

---

### Meaning
To notice, recognize, or understand something; to become aware of something through observation, experience, or thought.

**Part of Speech:** verb

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Daily Observation:**  
   "I could perceive the aroma of freshly baked bread from the bakery nearby."

2. **Workplace Interaction:**  
   "She perceived a change in her colleague's behavior after the meeting."

3. **Learning Environment:**  
   "Students perceive new concepts better when examples are used."

4. **Social Interaction:**  
   "He perceived her tone as being slightly annoyed."

5. **Travel Experience:**  
   "Travelers can perceive the cultural differences when visiting a new country."

6. **Technology Usage:**  
   "The camera can perceive motion and trigger the alarm automatically."

7. **Health Awareness:**  
   "Doctors can perceive early signs of illness through routine check-ups."

8. **Personal Insight:**  
   "She perceived the opportunity to improve her skills and enrolled in a course."

9. **Nature Observation:**  
   "We could perceive the subtle colors of the sunset."

10. **Everyday Life:**  
    "He perceived that the store was closing early due to a holiday."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **perceive** as **“per + ceive”** — to *fully grasp* or *take in* something through your senses or mind.

---

### Synonyms
notice, observe, discern, detect, recognize, understand, sense

### Antonyms
ignore, overlook, miss, disregard, neglect

---

### Word Forms
- **Perceived** (past tense): "She perceived a problem with the design."
- **Perceiving** (present participle): "He is perceiving subtle differences in the colors."
- **Perception** (noun): "His perception of the situation helped him make a decision."

---

### Common Phrases
- Perceive a change  
- Perceive an opportunity  
- Perceive the difference  
- Perceive the environment  
- Perceive through senses
