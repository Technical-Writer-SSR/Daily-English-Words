# Daily English Words Repository

**Date:** 01 January 2026  
**Word of the Day:** **Reflexive** (/rɪˈflek.sɪv/) • adjective  

**Meaning:**  
Done automatically or without conscious thought; relating to a reflex or a habitual response.

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

- **Instant Reaction:** "Pulling your hand away from a hot surface is a reflexive action."
- **Conversation:** "Saying 'thank you' after help has become a reflexive habit for her."
- **Safety Response:** "Wearing a seatbelt has become a reflexive behavior for him."
- **Workplace:** "He gave a reflexive reply before fully thinking about the question."
- **Driving:** "Braking suddenly to avoid an accident is a reflexive response."
- **Sports:** "The goalkeeper made a reflexive save when the ball came unexpectedly."
- **Health:** "Blinking when something moves toward your eyes is reflexive."
- **Daily Routine:** "Checking his phone after waking up is now reflexive."
- **Learning Process:** "With practice, correct typing becomes reflexive."
- **Emergency Situation:** "Her reflexive shout warned others of the danger."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **reflexive** as **“reflex + ive”** — actions your body or mind does *automatically*.

---

### Synonyms
automatic, instinctive, involuntary, habitual, spontaneous  

### Antonyms
deliberate, conscious, intentional, planned  

---

### Word Forms
- **Reflexively** (adverb): "He reflexively stepped back."  
- **Reflex** (noun): "Touching fire triggers a reflex."  

---

### Common Phrases
- Reflexive response  
- Reflexive action  
- Reflexive behavior  
- Reflexive reaction
