## Word of the Day: **Acquire**
/əˈkwaɪər/ • verb  

To obtain, gain, or get something, often through effort, learning, or experience.

---

### Meaning
To obtain or gain something; to come into possession of; to learn or develop a skill or habit.

**Part of Speech:** verb

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Education:**  
   "She acquired new knowledge by attending the online course."

2. **Workplace Skills:**  
   "He acquired programming skills through months of practice."

3. **Business:**  
   "The company acquired a smaller startup to expand its market."

4. **Personal Growth:**  
   "Through traveling, he acquired a better understanding of different cultures."

5. **Finance:**  
   "Investors acquired shares in the new company."

6. **Daily Life:**  
   "I acquired a taste for coffee after trying it several times."

7. **Technology:**  
   "The software acquired the data from multiple sensors."

8. **Health & Fitness:**  
   "She acquired a habit of daily exercise for better health."

9. **Social Skills:**  
   "He acquired confidence after giving several presentations."

10. **Hobby/Interest:**  
    "I acquired a collection of rare stamps over the years."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **“acquire”** as **“a + quire”** — *actively seeking to get or gather* something.

---

### Synonyms
obtain, gain, get, earn, procure, achieve, learn, develop

### Antonyms
lose, give up, relinquish, forfeit, miss

---

### Word Forms
- **Acquired** (past tense): "She acquired new skills last year."
- **Acquiring** (present participle): "He is acquiring knowledge through reading."
- **Acquisition** (noun): "The acquisition of the new software improved productivity."

---

### Common Phrases
- Acquire skills
- Acquire knowledge
- Acquire experience
- Acquire assets
- Acquire a taste
