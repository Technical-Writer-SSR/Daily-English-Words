## Word of the Day: **Culinary**
/ˈkʌl.ɪ.nər.i/ • adjective  

Related to cooking or the kitchen; connected with food preparation.

---

### Meaning
Related to cooking, food preparation, or the kitchen.

**Part of Speech:** adjective

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Home Cooking:**  
   "She enjoys trying new culinary techniques while cooking dinner."

2. **Professional Kitchen:**  
   "He gained culinary experience while working in a hotel kitchen."

3. **Travel Experience:**  
   "Exploring local culinary traditions was the highlight of the trip."

4. **Education:**  
   "She enrolled in a culinary course to improve her cooking skills."

5. **Restaurant Review:**  
   "The restaurant is famous for its unique culinary style."

6. **Family Gathering:**  
   "The wedding featured a rich culinary menu."

7. **Media & TV Shows:**  
   "He watches culinary shows to learn new recipes."

8. **Business Context:**  
   "The city promotes its culinary heritage to attract tourists."

9. **Everyday Conversation:**  
   "Indian cuisine offers a wide range of culinary delights."

10. **Personal Interest:**  
    "Cooking has become his favorite culinary hobby."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **culinary** as coming from **“culina”** (Latin for kitchen).

---

### Synonyms
cooking-related, gastronomic, kitchen-related, food-based

### Antonyms
non-culinary, inedible, unrelated to cooking

---

### Word Forms
- **Cuisine** (noun): "Italian cuisine is popular worldwide."
- **Culinarily** (adverb): "The dish was culinarily impressive." *(less common)*

---

### Common Phrases
- Culinary skills
- Culinary arts
- Culinary tradition
- Culinary experience
- Culinary culture
