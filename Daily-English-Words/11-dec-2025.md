## Word of the Day: **Gratitude**
/ˈɡræt.ɪ.tuːd/ • noun  

The feeling or quality of being thankful; readiness to show appreciation and return kindness.

---

### Meaning
A warm sense of thankfulness or appreciation for something received or experienced.

**Part of Speech:** noun

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Personal Life:**  
   "She expressed her gratitude to her neighbor for helping with groceries."

2. **Workplace:**  
   "The manager sent an email to show gratitude for the team’s hard work."

3. **Education:**  
   "Students wrote letters of gratitude to their teachers at the end of the semester."

4. **Healthcare:**  
   "Patients expressed gratitude to the nurses for their care during recovery."

5. **Social Interactions:**  
   "He nodded in gratitude after receiving directions from a stranger."

6. **Family Life:**  
   "Parents feel gratitude when their children show kindness."

7. **Travel:**  
   "He left a note of gratitude for the hotel staff."

8. **Community Service:**  
   "Volunteers receive gratitude from those they help in the community."

9. **Special Occasions:**  
   "She gave a speech expressing gratitude on her retirement day."

10. **Daily Life:**  
    "I feel gratitude every morning for my health and family."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **gratitude** as **“grate + attitude”** — having a positive attitude toward what you receive.

---

### Synonyms
thankfulness, appreciation, recognition, acknowledgment, gratefulness

### Antonyms
ingratitude, ungratefulness, disregard, neglect

---

### Word Forms
- **Grateful** (adjective): "I am grateful for your support."  
- **Gratefully** (adverb): "She accepted the gift gratefully."  
- **Gratitudes** (plural noun): "She sent her gratitudes to everyone who helped."

---

### Common Phrases
- Express gratitude  
- Feel gratitude  
- Show gratitude  
- Gratitude journal  
- Heartfelt gratitude
