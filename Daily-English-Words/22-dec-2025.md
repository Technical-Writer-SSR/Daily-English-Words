## Word of the Day: **Commit**
/kəˈmɪt/ • verb  

To decide or promise to do something; to carry out an action; to dedicate oneself.

---

### Meaning
To promise or dedicate oneself to a cause or action; to carry out or perform something; to formally record or make permanent.

**Part of Speech:** verb

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Work Responsibility:**  
   "She committed to finishing the report before the deadline."

2. **Personal Decision:**  
   "He decided to commit to a daily exercise routine."

3. **Team Project:**  
   "All members committed their time to complete the project successfully."

4. **Technology (Git):**  
   "After fixing the bug, I committed the changes to the Git repository."

5. **Learning Habit:**  
   "She committed herself to improving her English communication skills."

6. **Relationship:**  
   "They are ready to commit to each other."

7. **Office Communication:**  
   "Once you commit to a plan, you should follow it properly."

8. **Legal Context:**  
   "He committed the task according to company policy."

9. **Education:**  
   "Students must commit enough time to revision before exams."

10. **Daily Discipline:**  
    "He committed to waking up early every morning."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **commit** as **“come + it”** — when you *come* to a decision, you *commit* to it fully.

---

### Synonyms
pledge, dedicate, promise, devote, carry out, perform

### Antonyms
withdraw, avoid, abandon, hesitate, refuse

---

### Word Forms
- **Committed** (past tense): "She committed to the project last week."
- **Committing** (present participle): "He is committing his time to learning Git."
- **Commitment** (noun): "Strong commitment leads to success."

---

### Common Phrases
- Commit to a plan
- Commit a change
- Commit a mistake
- Commit to memory
- Commit code
