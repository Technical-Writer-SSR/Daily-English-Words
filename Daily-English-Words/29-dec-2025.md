## Word of the Day: **Resistance**
/rɪˈzɪs.təns/ • noun  

The act of opposing or withstanding something; the ability to resist pressure, force, or change.

---

### Meaning
The act or power of opposing, withstanding, or struggling against something such as force, pressure, change, or authority.

**Part of Speech:** noun

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Health & Fitness:**  
   "Regular exercise helps build resistance to illness."

2. **Electrical Usage:**  
   "High resistance in the wire can reduce current flow."

3. **Workplace Change:**  
   "There was resistance from employees when the new policy was introduced."

4. **Medical Treatment:**  
   "The bacteria developed resistance to the antibiotic."

5. **Personal Habits:**  
   "He showed resistance to waking up early every morning."

6. **Social Situation:**  
   "The community faced resistance while trying to stop illegal construction."

7. **Learning Process:**  
   "Students sometimes show resistance to learning new subjects."

8. **Mechanical Context:**  
   "The door showed resistance when I tried to open it."

9. **Political Context:**  
   "The movement was a symbol of resistance against injustice."

10. **Technology:**  
    "Touchscreens may fail if there is too much electrical resistance."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **resistance** as **“re + insist”** — insisting *against* something.

---

### Synonyms
opposition, defiance, obstruction, endurance, firmness, refusal

### Antonyms
acceptance, compliance, cooperation, submission, agreement

---

### Word Forms
- **Resist** (verb): "She tried to resist the temptation."
- **Resisted** (past tense): "They resisted the pressure."
- **Resisting** (present participle): "He is resisting the change."

---

### Common Phrases
- Resistance training
- Electrical resistance
