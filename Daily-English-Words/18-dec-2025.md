## Word of the Day: **Crashes**
/kræʃɪz/ • noun / verb  

**As a noun:** Sudden failures or collisions.  
**As a verb (crash):** To collide, fail suddenly, or fall abruptly.

---

### Meaning
1. **Noun:** Sudden incidents of failure or collisions, such as computer system failures, vehicle accidents, or other sudden disruptions.  
2. **Verb:** To collide violently or to fail suddenly, often unexpectedly.

**Part of Speech:** noun / verb

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Computer Problems:**  
   "My laptop crashes whenever I open too many programs at once."

2. **Road Accidents:**  
   "The car crashes on icy roads can cause serious injuries."

3. **App Usage:**  
   "The game crashes frequently on older smartphones."

4. **Financial Context:**  
   "Stock market crashes can lead to major losses for investors."

5. **Air Travel:**  
   "Airplane crashes are rare but often receive major media coverage."

6. **Work Situation:**  
   "The server crashes disrupted the company’s online services for hours."

7. **Everyday Mishaps:**  
   "I nearly crashed into the mailbox while backing out of the driveway."

8. **Sports Incidents:**  
   "The cyclist crashes during the downhill race due to a sharp turn."

9. **System Monitoring:**  
   "Engine crashes in vehicles can indicate mechanical failures."

10. **Gaming Situation:**  
    "The video game crashes every time I reach level 10."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **crashes** as sudden impacts—either in systems, vehicles, or events—that stop normal operation.

---

### Synonyms
collision, accident, failure, breakdown, smash, stumble

### Antonyms
smooth operation, stability, success, safety, functioning

---

### Word Forms
- **Crash** (verb): "The website crashes often under heavy traffic."  
- **Crashed** (past tense): "The plane crashed due to poor visibility."  
- **Crashing** (present participle): "The servers are crashing repeatedly."

---

### Common Phrases
- Car crash  
- System crash
