## Word of the Day: **Transformative**
/trænsˈfɔːr.mə.tɪv/ • adjective  

Causing a major change or improvement; having the power to transform.

---

### Meaning
Causing a significant or fundamental change in form, appearance, nature, or character.

**Part of Speech:** adjective

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Career Growth:**  
   "Learning new technical skills was a transformative experience for my career."

2. **Education:**  
   "Online courses can be transformative for students who lack access to good teachers."

3. **Technology:**  
   "Artificial intelligence is playing a transformative role in modern industries."

4. **Personal Life:**  
   "Regular exercise had a transformative effect on his health."

5. **Workplace Change:**  
   "The new workflow brought transformative improvements in productivity."

6. **Healthcare:**  
   "Early diagnosis and treatment can be transformative for patients."

7. **Business Strategy:**  
   "Digital transformation proved transformative for the company."

8. **Social Impact:**  
   "Education has a transformative impact on society."

9. **Daily Habits:**  
   "Waking up early became a transformative habit for her lifestyle."

10. **Self-Development:**  
    "Reading one book a month had a transformative influence on his thinking."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **transformative** as **“transform + active”** — something that actively changes things in a big way.

---

### Synonyms
life-changing, revolutionary, groundbreaking, influential, impactful

### Antonyms
unchanging, minor, ineffective, stagnant, insignificant

---

### Word Forms
- **Transform** (verb): "Education can transform lives."
- **Transformed** (past tense): "He was transformed by the experience."
- **Transformation** (noun): "The project led to a complete transformation."

---

### Common Phrases
- Transformative experience
- Transformative change
- Transformative impact
- Transformative technology
