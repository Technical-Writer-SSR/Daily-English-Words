## Word of the Day: **Seize**
/siːz/ • verb  

To take hold of suddenly or forcibly; to grasp; to take possession of.

---

### Meaning
To take hold of suddenly or forcibly; to grasp; to take possession of; to capture or apprehend.

**Part of Speech:** verb

---

### Samples Situations

1. **Emergency Response:**  
   "When the fire alarm sounded, she had to seize her passport and laptop before evacuating."

2. **Business Strategy:**  
   "The startup decided to seize the market gap by launching before competitors could react."

3. **Law Enforcement:**  
   "Police were able to seize the illegal goods during the midnight warehouse raid."

4. **Personal Development:**  
   "He finally decided to seize the day and book that solo trip to Japan."

5. **Sports Action:**  
   "The goalkeeper managed to seize the ball just before it crossed the goal line."

6. **Political Context:**  
   "Rebels attempted to seize control of the capital during early morning hours."

7. **Medical Procedure:**  
   "Doctors had to seize the moment when the patient's vitals stabilized."

8. **Financial Wisdom:**  
   "Smart investors seize opportunities when stock prices drop significantly."

9. **Creative Process:**  
   "The writer would seize any moment of inspiration, even waking at 3 AM for ideas."

10. **Everyday Life:**  
    "I had to seize my dog's collar as he was about to chase a squirrel into traffic."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **"seize"** as **"see + ize"** — when you *see* an opportunity, you should *realize and act on it immediately*.

---

### Synonyms
grab, grasp, snatch, capture, apprehend, take, clutch

### Antonyms
release, let go, free, liberate, relinquish, surrender

---

### Word Forms
- **Seized** (past tense): "He seized the opportunity yesterday."
- **Seizing** (present participle): "She is seizing control of the situation."
- **Seizure** (noun): "The seizure of assets was ordered by the court."

---

### Common Phrases
- Seize the day (carpe diem)
- Seize control
- Seize an opportunity
- Seize the moment
