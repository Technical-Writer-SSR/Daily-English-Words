# Daily English Words Repository

**Date:** 08 December 2025  
**Word of the Day:** **Volunteer** (/ˌvɒl.ənˈtɪər/) • noun & verb  

**Meaning:**  
As a **noun**: A person who offers to do something willingly without being paid.  
As a **verb**: To offer to do something willingly without being forced.

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

- **Community Service:** "She decided to volunteer at the local animal shelter on weekends."  
- **Workplace Initiative:** "He volunteered to lead the charity drive at the office."  
- **School Activity:** "Many students volunteered to help organize the school festival."  
- **Medical Assistance:** "Doctors volunteered their time to treat patients during the health camp."  
- **Disaster Relief:** "People volunteered to distribute food after the flood."  
- **Social Event:** "He volunteered to manage the registration desk for the event."  
- **Environmental Effort:** "Citizens volunteered to clean up the park on Earth Day."  
- **Helping Seniors:** "She volunteered to visit the elderly in the care home."  
- **Team Sports:** "Players volunteered to coach younger kids in their free time."  
- **Personal Initiative:** "He volunteered for extra shifts to support the team during busy hours."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **volunteer** as *“voluntarily offering help”*—doing something willingly without being asked or paid.

---

### Synonyms
offer, enlist, contribute, help, participate  

### Antonyms
compel, force, oblige, refuse  

---

### Word Forms
- **Volunteered** (past tense): "She volunteered at the charity event yesterday."  
- **Volunteering** (present participle): "He is volunteering to teach coding to kids."  
- **Volunteer** (noun): "A volunteer helped guide visitors at the museum."

---

### Common Phrases
- Volunteer work  
- Volunteer for a cause  
- Community volunteer  
- Volunteer opportunity
