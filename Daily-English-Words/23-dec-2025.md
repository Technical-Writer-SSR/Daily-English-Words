## Word of the Day: **Reap**
/riːp/ • verb  

To harvest a crop; to receive or obtain something as a result of actions.

---

### Meaning
To obtain something, especially as a result of effort or work; to harvest or collect.

**Part of Speech:** verb

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Work Effort:**  
   "If you work hard consistently, you will reap the benefits in the long run."

2. **Education:**  
   "Students reap success when they prepare well for exams."

3. **Health & Fitness:**  
   "She started exercising daily to reap better health."

4. **Business:**  
   "The company hopes to reap profits after investing in new technology."

5. **Personal Growth:**  
   "He reaped confidence by practicing English every day."

6. **Farming Context:**  
   "Farmers reap the crops at the end of the season."

7. **Teamwork:**  
   "The team reaped good results because everyone worked together."

8. **Financial Planning:**  
   "Investors reap rewards when they make smart decisions."

9. **Learning Habit:**  
   "You reap knowledge by reading regularly."

10. **Daily Life:**  
    "She reaped satisfaction from helping others."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **reap** as **“repeat effort → positive result”**.

---

### Synonyms
harvest, gain, obtain, collect, earn, receive

### Antonyms
lose, forfeit, waste, miss, surrender

---

### Word Forms
- **Reaped** (past tense): "He reaped the reward of his hard work."
- **Reaping** (present participle): "She is reaping the benefits now."

---

### Common Phrases
- Reap the benefits
- Reap rewards
- Reap profits
- Reap success
- Reap what you sow
