## Word of the Day: **Longevity**
/lɒnˈdʒev.ɪ.ti/ • noun  

The length of time that someone or something continues to exist or function.

---

### Meaning
The long duration of life, existence, or usefulness; the ability to last for a long time.

**Part of Speech:** noun

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Health & Lifestyle:**  
   "Regular exercise and a balanced diet can improve longevity."

2. **Career Growth:**  
   "Skill upgrades help ensure longevity in a competitive job market."

3. **Technology Products:**  
   "This laptop is known for its battery longevity."

4. **Business Planning:**  
   "Customer satisfaction is key to the longevity of any company."

5. **Daily Habits:**  
   "Good sleep habits contribute to mental and physical longevity."

6. **Infrastructure:**  
   "Proper maintenance increases the longevity of buildings and roads."

7. **Electronics Usage:**  
   "Reducing overheating can extend the longevity of electronic devices."

8. **Family Life:**  
   "The grandparents are admired for their longevity and healthy lifestyle."

9. **Finance:**  
   "Long-term investments are planned for financial longevity."

10. **Engineering Context:**  
    "Material selection plays a major role in the longevity of mechanical parts."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **longevity** as **“long + gravity”** — something that *stays* for a long time.

---

### Synonyms
long life, durability, endurance, lifespan, permanence

### Antonyms
shortness, brevity, mortality, impermanence

---

### Word Forms
- **Long-lived** (adjective): "A long-lived product saves money."
- **Longevous** (adjective, rare): "Some species are longevous."

---

### Common Phrases
- Longevity of life
- Product longevity
- Career longevity
- Battery longevity
- Longevity studies
