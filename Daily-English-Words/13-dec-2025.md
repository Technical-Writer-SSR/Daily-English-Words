## Word of the Day: **Decline**
/dɪˈklaɪn/ • verb / noun  

**Verb:** To become smaller, weaker, or lower; to politely refuse.  
**Noun:** A gradual decrease or reduction.

---

### Meaning
- **Verb:** To decrease in strength, quality, or amount; to politely refuse something.  
- **Noun:** A reduction or downward trend in quantity, quality, or importance.

**Part of Speech:** verb / noun

---

### Sample Situations Used in Daily Life

1. **Health Context:**  
   "Her health began to decline after years of neglecting exercise."

2. **Business Performance:**  
   "Sales declined sharply during the last quarter."

3. **Social Invitation:**  
   "He politely declined the dinner invitation due to a prior commitment."

4. **Academic Performance:**  
   "The student's grades declined when he stopped attending classes regularly."

5. **Technology Use:**  
   "Battery performance can decline over time with frequent charging."

6. **Environmental Issue:**  
   "Forest areas have declined significantly over the past decade."

7. **Finance:**  
   "The value of the stock declined after negative market news."

8. **Daily Conversation:**  
   "She declined to comment on the ongoing debate."

9. **Sports:**  
   "The athlete's performance declined after the injury."

10. **Government / Policy:**  
    "Public trust in the administration showed a steady decline."

---

### Memory Tip
Think of **decline** as a slope — things either **go down** or you **turn down** something politely.

---

### Synonyms
decrease, reduce, drop, fall, refuse, reject, deteriorate

### Antonyms
increase, rise, improve, accept, strengthen

---

### Word Forms
- **Declined** (past tense): "He declined the offer yesterday."  
- **Declining** (present participle): "The company's profits are declining."  
- **Declines** (plural noun / verb): "There are noticeable declines in air quality."

---

### Common Phrases
- Decline an offer  
- Decline in health  
- Decline in sales  
- Steady decline  
- Gradual decline
